Chapter 1: The Forgotten Letter

    The envelope was yellowed, its edges frayed with age.

    Lydia traced her name—written in ink now faded—with a trembling finger.

    Rain tapped against the window as if urging her to open it.

    Inside, a single sentence: "They lied about the fire."

    Her breath caught; the fire had taken everything ten years ago.

    The signature was illegible, smudged by time—or tears.

    A knock at the door made her jump, the letter slipping to the floor.

    Through the peephole, a stranger stood holding a matching envelope.

    His eyes were hollow, as if he hadn’t slept in days.

    Before she could speak, he whispered, "You’re not supposed to remember."
